## <T en="Frogs?" ja="カエル？" en_simple="Frogs?" />

<p>
  <T
    en="Living in the Japanese countryside means my backyard is basically rice paddies. Rice paddies are full of water, so they attract lots of bugs, and frogs. Starting a month or two ago, the “frog times”(self created name) started. That's when I can hear the thousands or perhaps millions? Of frogs making their frog ribbit ribbit sounds every night. It's also when I start seeing them pop up on my kitchen windows. All those bugs drawn to the light inside my house become a buffet just waiting outside, and the frogs know it. They'll climb right up, sometimes sticking to the glass, other times just chilling on the tree next to the window."
    ja="日本の田舎に住んでいると、家の裏は基本的に田んぼです。田んぼには水がたくさんあるので、虫がたくさん集まり、そしてカエルもたくさん集まります。一、二ヶ月前から、僕が勝手に名付けた「カエルの季節」が始まりました。それは、毎晩何千、いや何百万ものカエルが「ゲロゲロ」と鳴く声が聞こえる時期のことです。キッチンの窓にカエルが現れ始めるのもこの頃です。家の中の明かりに引き寄せられた虫たちが、外で待っているカエルたちのごちそうになるのです。カエルたちはそれをよく知っています。彼らは壁をよじ登り、時にはガラスに張り付いたり、窓の隣の木でくつろいだりしています。"
    en_simple="I live in the countryside in Japan. My backyard is full of rice fields. Rice fields have a lot of water. The water brings bugs, and the bugs bring frogs. The bugs like the light from my house. The frogs wait outside to eat the bugs. They climb the walls, stick to the window, or just sit on a tree."
