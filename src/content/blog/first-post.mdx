## <T><span slot="en">Frogs?</span><span slot="ja">カエル？</span><span slot="en_simple">Frogs?</span></T>

<T>
  <span slot="en">
    Living in the Japanese countryside means my backyard is basically rice
    paddies. Rice paddies are full of water, so they attract lots of bugs, and
    frogs. Starting a month or two ago, the “frog times”(self created name)
    started. That's when I can hear the thousands or perhaps millions? Of frogs
    making their frog ribbit ribbit sounds every night. It's also when I start
    seeing them pop up on my kitchen windows. All those bugs drawn to the light
    inside my house become a buffet just waiting outside, and the frogs know it.
    They'll climb right up, sometimes sticking to the glass, other times just
    chilling on the tree next to the window.
  </span>
  <span slot="ja">
    日本の田舎に住んでいると、家の裏は基本的に田んぼです。田んぼには水がたくさんあるので、虫がたくさん集まり、そしてカエルもたくさん集まります。一、二ヶ月前から、僕が勝手に名付けた「カエルの季節」が始まりました。それは、毎晩何千、いや何百万ものカエルが「ゲロゲロ」と鳴く声が聞こえる時期のことです。キッチンの窓にカエルが現れ始めるのもこの頃です。家の中の明かりに引き寄せられた虫たちが、外で待っているカエルたちのごちそうになるのです。カエルたちはそれをよく知っています。彼らは壁をよじ登り、時にはガラスに張り付いたり、窓の隣の木でくつろいだりしています。
  </span>
  <span slot="en_simple">
    I live in the countryside in Japan. My backyard is full of rice fields. Rice
    fields have a lot of water. The water brings bugs, and the bugs bring frogs.
    About a month ago, the 'frog times' started. This is when I can hear
    thousands of frogs making 'ribbit ribbit' sounds every night. It is also
    when I see them on my kitchen windows. The bugs like the light from my
    house. The frogs wait outside to eat the bugs. They climb the walls, stick
    to the window, or just sit on a tree.
  </span>
</T>

### <T><span slot="en">A case of mistaken identity</span><span slot="ja">虫違い</span><span slot="en_simple">Mistaken identity</span></T>

<T>
  <span slot="en">
    On this day however, as I was walking through my kitchen in the dark getting
    ready to go to sleep I felt something brush against my foot. At first I
    thought it was a house centipede, since I get a lot of those in my house,
    and I rushed for my bug spray. I usually let them live since they eat other
    bugs, but this one started me and I needed revenge.
  </span>
  <span slot="ja">
    しかし、その日は、暗いキッチンを歩いて寝ようとしていた時、何かが足に触れました。最初、それはゲジゲジだと思いました。家にはよく出るので。普段は他の虫を食べてくれるので生かしておくのですが、この時はびっくりさせられたので、仕返しが必要でした。僕は急いで虫スプレーを取りに行きました。
  </span>
  <span slot="en_simple">
    One night, I was walking in my dark kitchen. I was getting ready for bed.
    Something touched my foot. At first, I thought it was a house centipede. I
    see them a lot. I usually let them live because they eat other bugs. But
    this time, it scared me. I wanted revenge. I went to get my bug spray.
  </span>
</T>

### <T><span slot="en">The culprit revealed</span><span slot="ja">犯人判明</span><span slot="en_simple">The culprit!</span></T>

<T>
  <span slot="en">
    When I flicked on the light, ready for battle, I found it wasn’t an
    alien-looking centipede at all. It was a tiny, perfect, and very cute
    Japanese Tree Frog. I have no idea how it got in, but I'm guessing it
    climbed the overgrown tree next to my house and slipped in through the
    ventilation fan.
  </span>
  <span slot="ja">
    戦いの準備を整え、電気をつけると、そこにいたのはエイリアンのようなゲジゲジではなく、小さくて完璧で、とてもかわいいニホンアマガエルでした。どうやって入ってきたのか全く分かりませんが、たぶん、家の隣の伸びすぎた木を登って、換気扇から滑り込んだのでしょう。
  </span>
  <span slot="en_simple">
    I turned on the light. I was ready to fight. But it was not a scary bug. It
    was a small, perfect, and very cute Japanese Tree Frog. I do not know how it
    got inside. I think it climbed a tree and came in through a fan vent.
  </span>
</T>

### <T><span slot="en">A Fleeting Dream of Pet Ownership</span><span slot="ja">ペットオーナーという儚い夢</span><span slot="en_simple">A Short Dream of Having a Pet</span></T>

<T>
  <span slot="en">
    "You have to catch it!" said my girlfriend over the phone. Taking her
    advice, I gently coaxed the frog into a glass jar.My future with this little
    guy flashed before my eyes. I imagined him getting bigger and bigger. Would
    I need to buy an aquarium? How long do frogs even live? I named him froggy.
  </span>
  <span slot="ja">
    「捕まえて！」と、その時電話していた彼女が言いました。そのアドバイスに従い、僕はそっとカエルをガラスの瓶に入れました。この小さな相棒との未来が目の前に広がりました。彼がどんどん大きくなるのを想像しました。水槽を買う必要があるかな？カエルってどのくらい生きるんだろう？僕は彼を「カエルくん」と名付けました。
  </span>
  <span slot="en_simple">
    "You have to catch it!" said my girlfriend over the phone. So, I put the
    frog in a glass jar. I thought about my future with this little frog. I
    imagined him getting bigger. Would I need to buy a tank? How long do frogs
    even live? I named him froggy.
  </span>
</T>

<T>
  <span slot="en">
    Unfortunately my dreams were shattered almost immediately after my
    girlfriend read the google search result “they only eat live food”. The
    reality of trying to catch live flies for the froggy hit me. I realized I
    was far too lazy to raise a frog. I took him outside and released him into
    the darkness to hopefully fight another day, and more importantly, not get
    eaten by the stray cat I’d seen earlier.
  </span>
  <span slot="ja">
    残念ながら、その夢は彼女がGoogleの検索結果を読み上げた直後に打ち砕かれました。「生きた餌しか食べないんだって」。カエルくんのために生きたハエを捕まえるという現実が、僕に重くのしかかりました。僕はカエルを育てるには怠けすぎると気づきました。彼を外に連れて行き、暗闇に放してあげました。うまくいけば、彼はまた別の日を戦い抜き、そして、その日早くに見かけた野良猫に食べられないことを祈ります。
  </span>
  <span slot="en_simple">
    Sadly, my dream ended very fast. My girlfriend read something from Google.
    She said, “they only eat live food.” I thought about catching live flies for
    froggy. I knew I was too lazy to raise a frog. I took him outside and let
    him go into the dark. I hope he will be okay, and I hope the stray cat I saw
    earlier did not eat him.
  </span>
</T>
